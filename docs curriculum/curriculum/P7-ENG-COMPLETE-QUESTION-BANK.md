# Canonical P7 English Language (NCDC & UNEB PLE) Complete Question Bank

This canonical document contains the exhaustive, progressive multi-module question bank for **Primary 7 English Language (`p7-eng`)** in Elimu. It covers 100% of all 4 canonical Uganda National Curriculum Development Centre (NCDC) and Primary Leaving Examinations (PLE) syllabus sections (`Punctuation & Vocabulary`, `Comprehensive Grammar & Sentence Structures`, `Reading Comprehension`, and `PLE Composition & Functional Writing`), structured into **4 Master Topics** across **16 Bite-Sized Modules (`195 Progressive Questions Total`)** with strict Ugandan context (`zero repetitive questions, authentic PLE multi-step reasoning, NCDC vocabulary, and exact UNEB past-paper alignment`).

---

## Complete Topic & Module Roadmap (`4 Topics`, `16 Modules`, `195 Questions`)

### 1. Punctuation, Vocabulary, Spelling & Affixes (`p7-eng-grammar-vocab`) — 4 Modules (`49 Questions`)
- `p7-gvc-m1`: Module 1: Punctuation Marks, Apostrophes & Capitalization (`12 questions`: proper nouns capitalization, full stops, question marks, exclamation marks, commas, possessive apostrophes `James's book / pupils' bags` vs contractions `can't, it's`, quotation marks/inverted commas `"..."`, colons, semicolons).
- `p7-gvc-m2`: Module 2: Synonyms, Antonyms, Homophones & Homographs (`12 questions`: synonyms `purchase/buy, assist/help`, antonyms `generous/stingy, permanent/temporary, postpone/bring forward`, homophones `there/their/they're, stationary/stationery, peace/piece, weather/whether, sight/site/cite`, homographs `tear/tear`).
- `p7-gvc-m3`: Module 3: Prefixes, Suffixes & Word Formation (`12 questions`: prefixes `un-, dis-, in-, il-, im-, ir-, mis-, non-, pre-, re-`, suffixes `-able, -ible, -ful, -less, -ness, -ment, -ship, -tion, -sion`, transforming verbs/adjectives into abstract nouns `decide -> decision, honest -> honesty, proud -> pride`).
- `p7-gvc-m4`: Module 4: Abbreviations, Collective Nouns & Plurals (`13 questions`: standard abbreviations `e.g., i.e., vs., P.T.O., R.S.V.P., NCDC, UNEB, UPDF`, irregular plurals `child/children, ox/oxen, sheep/sheep, tooth/teeth, mouse/mice`, compound plurals `mothers-in-law, passers-by`, Latin/foreign plurals `radius/radii, nucleus/nuclei, fungus/fungi, crisis/crises, datum/data, oasis/oases`, collective nouns `swarm of bees, pride of lions, school of dolphins, troop of monkeys, fleet of ships`).

### 2. Comprehensive English Grammar & Sentence Structures (`p7-eng-grammar-structures`) — 5 Modules (`61 Questions`)
- `p7-gms-m1`: Module 1: Tenses, Irregular Verbs & Verb Structures (`12 questions`: Present/Past/Future Simple, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous; irregular verb conjugations `write/wrote/written, burst/burst/burst, hang/hung/hung vs hanged, fly/flew/flown, ring/rang/rung, sink/sank/sunk, lie/lay/lain`, subject-verb agreement `neither...nor`).
- `p7-gms-m2`: Module 2: Direct and Indirect (Reported) Speech (`12 questions`: tense backshifting `Present Simple -> Past Simple, Present Continuous -> Past Continuous`, time/place pronoun shifts `now -> then, today -> that day, tomorrow -> the next day, here -> there, ago -> before`, reporting questions and commands/imperatives `warned them not to play`).
- `p7-gms-m3`: Module 3: Active and Passive Voice & Question Tags (`12 questions`: transforming active into passive voice `Subject -> by + Object; Verb -> to be + Past Participle: will present -> will be presented`, positive vs negative question tags `Akello is our monitor, isn't she?` / `Shut the door, will you?` / `I am early, aren't I?` / `Let us go, shall we?`).
- `p7-gms-m4`: Module 4: Conditionals ('If' Clauses) & Relative Clauses (`12 questions`: Conditional Type 1 `If + Present Simple, + Future Simple`, Conditional Type 2 `If + Past Simple, + would + bare infinitive: If I were`, Conditional Type 3 `If + Past Perfect, + would have + Past Participle: would have been`, relative pronouns `who, whom, whose, which, that, where, when`, inverted structures `No sooner had... than`).
- `p7-gms-m5`: Module 5: Adjectives, Adverbs, Prepositions & Phrasal Verbs (`13 questions`: degrees of comparison `tall/taller/tallest, good/better/best, far/farther/farthest`, OSASCOMP order of adjectives `A beautiful small new round red Ugandan leather traveling bag`, fixed preposition collocations `accuse of, apologize for, arrive at/in, depend on, prefer to, suffer from`, phrasal verbs `break down, call off, put off, look forward to, take after, turn down`).

### 3. Reading Comprehension & Critical Analysis (`p7-eng-comprehension`) — 3 Modules (`37 Questions`)
- `p7-cmp-m1`: Module 1: Continuous Passages (`Stories, Biographies & Reports`) (`12 questions`: interpreting narrative texts about Ugandan primary school life, historical accounts like Dr. David Livingstone, identifying main ideas, inferential vocabulary reasoning `burning the midnight oil, hit the nail on the head`).
- `p7-cmp-m2`: Module 2: Non-Continuous Passages (`Timetables, Charts & Advertisements`) (`12 questions`: reading bus/train timetables from Kampala to Gulu/Mbarara, calculating transit durations and stopover rest times, restaurant menus and price lists, school public notices, calendar schedules, job advertisements and deadlines).
- `p7-cmp-m3`: Module 3: Poetry Interpretation & Literary Devices (`13 questions`: analyzing three-stanza poems about Ugandan nature/education/patriotism, identifying stanzas, rhyme schemes `A-A-A`, similes `as silver pearls / as brave as a lion / as white as snow`, metaphors `she is a shining star`, personification `the River Nile roars like an angry lion`).

### 4. PLE Composition, Formal Letter Writing & Functional Writing (`p7-eng-composition`) — 4 Modules (`48 Questions`)
- `p7-com-m1`: Module 1: Formal Official Letters vs Informal Personal Letters (`12 questions`: two addresses top right and left vs one address, salutations `Dear Sir/Madam,` vs `Dear Mr. Musoke,` vs `Dear Uncle Kato,`, reference/subject line `REF: APPLICATION FOR...`, complimentary close `Yours faithfully,` vs `Yours sincerely,` vs `Your loving son,`, signature + printed name).
- `p7-com-m2`: Module 2: Functional Writing (`Notices, Announcements & Telegrams/SMS`) (`12 questions`: drafting school public notices with the 5 Ws (`What, When, Where, Who, Issuer`), official lost-and-found announcements, wedding/graduation invitations and R.S.V.P. acceptance/declining cards, concise telegram/SMS messaging `ARRIVED SAFELY SCHOOL GULU 2PM ALL WELL`).
- `p7-com-m3`: Module 3: Minutes of Meetings & Dialogue/Script Writing (`12 questions`: structure of formal meeting minutes `Heading, Attendance present/apologies, Agenda, Min 1 Communication from Chair, Min 2 Matters Arising, A.O.B., Adjournment, Signatures`, exact formatting of dialogues with colons after speaker names without quotation marks).
- `p7-com-m4`: Module 4: Picture & Guided Composition (`Section B Story Writing`) (`12 questions`: interpreting four-picture and six-picture sequences, chronological storytelling, paragraphing, cohesive transitional devices `suddenly, meanwhile, consequently, finally`, avoiding slang and mother-tongue direct translation `hear a smell`, ending with proverbs).
