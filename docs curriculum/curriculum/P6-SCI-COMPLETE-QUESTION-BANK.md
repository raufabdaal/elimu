# Canonical P6 Integrated Science (NCDC Curriculum) Complete Question Bank

This canonical document contains the exhaustive, progressive multi-module question bank for **Primary 6 Integrated Science (`p6-sci`)** in Elimu. It covers 100% of all 8 canonical Uganda National Curriculum Development Centre (NCDC) syllabus units, structured into **8 Master Topics** across **26 Bite-Sized Modules (`311 Progressive Questions Total`)** with strict Ugandan and East African context (`zero repetitive questions, authentic P6/PLE multi-step reasoning, NCDC vocabulary, and exact past-paper alignment`).

## Complete Topic & Module Roadmap (`8 Topics`, `26 Modules`, `311 Questions`)

### 1. Classification of Animals (`Vertebrates & Invertebrates`) (`p6-sci-animals`) — 4 Modules (`48 Questions`)
- `p6-ani-m1`: Module 1: Mammals & Birds (`Warm-Blooded Vertebrates`) (`12 questions`: homoiothermic vs poikilothermic, fur/hair, viviparous vs oviparous, mammary glands, blowholes in whales, monotremes `platypus/echidna`, marsupials `kangaroos`; bird feather types `quill/flight, contour, down, filoplume`, beaks and feet adaptations `birds of prey talons, ducks webbed feet, sunbirds probing`).
- `p6-ani-m2`: Module 2: Reptiles, Amphibians & Fish (`Cold-Blooded Vertebrates`) (`12 questions`: ectothermic behavioral adaptations, dry horny scales vs smooth moist skin vs wet slimy overlapping scales; internal vs external fertilization; chameleon sticky tongue/color change; fish operculum, paired fins `pectoral/pelvic` vs median fins `dorsal/anal/caudal`, swim bladder; frog metamorphosis `eggs/spawn -> gill-breathing tadpole -> lung/skin adult`).
- `p6-ani-m3`: Module 3: Arthropods - Insects, Arachnids, Crustaceans & Myriapods (`12 questions`: chitin exoskeleton ecdysis/molting; Class Insecta `3 segments, 6 legs, antennae, wings, spiracles/trachea`, complete vs incomplete metamorphosis; Class Arachnida `2 segments, 8 legs, no wings/antennae`; Class Crustacea `carapace, 10 legs pincers, aquatic gills`; Class Myriapoda `centipedes 1 pair legs/segment poison claws vs millipedes 2 pairs legs/segment`; termite castes `Queen, King, sterile Soldiers, Workers`).
- `p6-ani-m4`: Module 4: Other Invertebrates (`Molluscs, Annelids, Coelenterates & Protozoa`) (`12 questions`: Phylum Mollusca `garden snail spiral shell, radula rasping tongue, aestivation/epiphragm, bivalves, cephalopods`; Phylum Annelida `earthworms chaetae/clitellum aerating soil/vermicompost vs leeches hirudin anti-coagulant`; Phylum Coelenterata `Hydra/jellyfish stinging nematocysts`; Phylum Protozoa `single-celled Amoeba pseudopodia, Paramecium cilia, Plasmodium`).

### 2. Classification of Plants (`Flowering vs Non-Flowering`) (`p6-sci-plants`) — 3 Modules (`36 Questions`)
- `p6-pla-m1`: Module 1: Monocots vs Dicots (`Flowering Plants / Angiosperms`) (`12 questions`: Monocots `1 cotyledon, fibrous roots, parallel venation, flower parts in 3s, scattered vascular bundles without cambium e.g. maize, rice, wheat, banana` vs Dicots `2 cotyledons, taproot system, network venation, ring vascular bundles with cambium allowing woody tree growth e.g. beans, peas, mango, coffee`; xylem vs phloem translocation).
- `p6-pla-m2`: Module 2: Structure of Flowers, Pollination & Fertilization (`12 questions`: stamen `anther, filament` vs carpel/pistil `stigma, style, ovary`; self vs cross pollination `hybrid vigor`; insect-pollinated `entomophilous - bright petals, sweet scent, nectar, sticky pollen` vs wind-pollinated `anemophilous - dull petals, no scent/nectar, feathery stigmas, dangling anthers, dry pollen e.g. maize silks`; fertilization micropyle, seed and fruit formation, dispersal methods).
- `p6-pla-m3`: Module 3: Seed Germination (`Epigeal vs Hypogeal`) & Non-Flowering Plants (`12 questions`: germination conditions `moisture, warmth, oxygen`; Epigeal `hypocotyl elongates, cotyledons pushed above ground e.g. beans, sunflower` vs Hypogeal `epicotyl elongates, cotyledons stay underground e.g. maize, peas`; Cryptogams: Ferns `spores in sori on leaves`, Mosses `rhizoids`, Fungi `mushrooms/yeast lacking chlorophyll heterotrophs`, Conifers `naked seeds in woody cones`).

### 3. Sound, Light & Heat Energy (`Physical Science & Propagation`) (`p6-sci-energy-heat`) — 3 Modules (`36 Questions`)
- `p6-enh-m1`: Module 1: Heat Energy - Sources, Modes of Transfer (`Conduction/Convection/Radiation`) (`12 questions`: heat Joules vs temperature °C/K; Conduction `vibrating solid particles in metals vs insulators`, Convection `rising fluid currents in boiling water, room ventilators, land/sea breezes`, Radiation `infrared waves across vacuum from Sun, campfire`; double-walled Thermos flask silvered glass and vacuum gap; petrol tanker silver paint; thermal expansion of rails and anomalous expansion of water `4°C to 0°C`).
- `p6-enh-m2`: Module 2: Thermometers, Temperature Scales & Thermal Expansion Applications (`12 questions`: mercury/alcohol liquids; Clinical thermometer `kink/constriction, 35°C to 42°C` vs Laboratory thermometer `-10°C to 110°C` vs Six's Max/Min; cleaning clinical thermometers with alcohol; telephone wire sag; bimetallic strip thermostat; Celsius fixed points `0°C and 100°C`).
- `p6-enh-m3`: Module 3: Sound & Light Energy Fundamentals (`Production & Propagation`) (`12 questions`: sound produced by vibrations, requires a medium `fastest in steel, cannot travel in vacuum`, pitch `frequency Hz` vs volume `amplitude`, echoes `echolocation in bats/dolphins, SONAR`, rectilinear propagation of light `pinhole cameras`, luminous vs non-luminous, regular reflection $i=r$ vs diffuse reflection, periscope, lateral inversion, dispersion of white light through glass prism into ROYGBIV spectrum/rainbow, refraction/apparent depth, primary colors Red/Green/Blue).

### 4. Simple Electric Circuits & Magnetism (`p6-sci-electricity-magnetism`) — 3 Modules (`34 Questions`)
- `p6-elm-m1`: Module 1: Electric Circuits, Cells (`Series vs Parallel`) & Conductors (`12 questions`: dry cell chemical-to-electrical energy, switch/key, series loop vs parallel home wiring, fuse safety wire, ammeter/voltmeter, good conductors `copper, graphite, salty water` vs insulators `rubber, PVC, dry wood`).
- `p6-elm-m2`: Module 2: Static Electricity, Lightning & Lightning Conductors (`10 questions`: static friction charging `like charges repel, unlike attract`, electroscope, lightning spark between clouds/earth, thunder shockwave, lightning arrestor copper spikes and earthing in moist soil, squatting low in open pitches).
- `p6-elm-m3`: Module 3: Properties & Uses of Magnets and Electromagnets (`12 questions`: freely suspended magnet North-South alignment, like poles repel/unlike attract, ferromagnetic `iron, steel, cobalt, nickel`, stroking and electrical magnetization methods, demagnetizing via heat/dropping/AC, soft iron cores in electric bells and scrap lifting cranes vs hard steel permanent magnets, magnetic keepers).

### 5. Respiratory & Digestive Systems of Humans (`p6-sci-body-systems`) — 3 Modules (`36 Questions`)
- `p6-bod-m1`: Module 1: The Human Respiratory System (`Breathing & Gaseous Exchange`) (`12 questions`: nose cilia/mucus warming/filtering air, epiglottis, trachea C-shaped cartilage rings, bronchi/bronchioles, alveoli gaseous exchange across one-cell walls; inhalation intercostal contraction/diaphragm flattening vs exhalation; inhaled `21% O2, 0.04% CO2` vs exhaled `16% O2, 4% CO2`; respiratory diseases `TB BCG/DOTS, pneumonia, asthma, lung cancer from tobacco tar/nicotine`, lime water test).
- `p6-bod-m2`: Module 2: The Human Digestive System (`Alimentary Canal & Enzymes`) (`12 questions`: mechanical vs chemical digestion; enzymes: mouth salivary amylase/ptyalin for cooked starch $\to$ maltose, stomach HCl activating pepsin for proteins $\to$ peptides (`rennin curdling milk`), duodenum receiving liver bile (`emulsifying fats`) and pancreatic juice (`trypsin, amylase, lipase`), ileum intestinal juice enzymes and absorption via millions of Villi (`blood capillaries and lacteals`); colon water reabsorption, roughage/fiber preventing constipation, peptic ulcers).
- `p6-bod-m3`: Module 3: Human Teeth, Dental Hygiene & Digestive System Diseases (`12 questions`: 32 permanent teeth vs 20 milk teeth; incisors biting, canines tearing, premolars/molars grinding; tooth layers `enamel, dentine, pulp cavity nerves/blood, cement`; tooth decay `lactic acid from plaque bacteria`, brushing with fluoride and Vitamin C preventing scurvy; roundworms `Ascaris`, tapeworms, hookworms, appendicitis, mass de-worming).

### 6. Health, Sanitation, Hygiene & First Aid (`p6-sci-health-sanitation`) — 3 Modules (`36 Questions`)
- `p6-san-m1`: Module 1: Sanitation, Latrines (`VIP`) & Waste Management (`3 Rs`) (`12 questions`: VIP latrine screened vent pipe drawing odors upward and starving flies, siting 30m downhill from water boreholes, Tippy-Tap foot pedal handwashing with soap, sorting biodegradable organic compost vs non-biodegradable polythene bags `kaveera` for 3 Rs `Reduce, Reuse, Recycle`, sunlit dish racks killing germs via UV rays, covering drinking water pots).
- `p6-san-m2`: Module 2: Communicable vs Non-Communicable Diseases & Germ Theory (`12 questions`: communicable `malaria, cholera, TB, measles MR vaccine` vs non-communicable `anemia, rickets, asthma, hypertension`; four modes of spread `airborne, waterborne fecal-oral, vector-borne, direct/blood contact`; kwashiorkor protein deficiency vs marasmus total starvation, goiter iodine deficiency; natural vs artificial immunity, UNEPI immunization schedule `BCG, Pentavalent, OPV, Measles-Rubella`).
- `p6-san-m3`: Module 3: First Aid Essentials (`Bleeding, Burns, Fractures, Snakebites & CPR`) (`12 questions`: 3 Ps `Preserve life, Prevent worsening, Promote recovery`; first aid box `gloves, gauze, crepe bandages, antiseptic, ORS`; direct pressure and elevation for severe arterial bleeding; burns dry heat vs scalds moist heat $\to$ cold running water 15-20 mins without popping blisters; CPR mouth-to-mouth for drowning; recovery position for unconscious fainting/epileptic casualties; stop, drop and roll for burning clothes; sideways scraping of bee stingers).

### 7. Interdependence of Things & Environmental Protection (`p6-sci-ecosystem-environment`) — 3 Modules (`36 Questions`)
- `p6-eco-m1`: Module 1: Biotic vs Abiotic Components & Photosynthesis (`12 questions`: biotic living vs abiotic physical factors `sunlight, water, soil, air`; photosynthesis manufacturing glucose using carbon dioxide, water, sunlight, and chlorophyll while releasing oxygen; autotrophs vs heterotrophs/decomposers; iodine starch test `blue-black`).
- `p6-eco-m2`: Module 2: Food Chains, Food Webs & Symbiotic Relationships (`12 questions`: arrows showing energy flow; producers, primary consumers/herbivores, secondary/tertiary carnivores, decomposers; 10% energy transfer law; scavengers `vultures/hyenas`, omnivores `humans/pigs`; mutualism `Rhizobium bacteria fixing nitrogen in bean root nodules, oxpecker birds`, parasitism `tapeworms, ticks, dodder plant Cuscuta`, commensalism `epiphytic orchids`, competition; biomagnification of DDT in top predators).
- `p6-eco-m3`: Module 3: Environmental Protection, Deforestation & Waste Management (`3 Rs`) (`12 questions`: deforestation topsoil erosion and climate change; afforestation vs re-afforestation, agroforestry `Grevillea/Calliandra among crops`, wetland/swamp protection NEMA, avoiding bush burning, 3 Rs, organic compost manure preparation (`4 steps`)).

### 8. Agriculture, Farm Management & Apiculture (`p6-sci-agriculture-livestock`) — 4 Modules (`49 Questions`)
- `p6-agr-m1`: Module 1: Soil Science, Soil Types & Components (`12 questions`: loam soil ideal for crops due to humus and balanced drainage; clay soil with smallest particles and high capillarity/water retention used for pottery/bricks; sand soil with large air spaces and fast drainage; earthworms aerating topsoil; soil profile horizons `A Topsoil, B Subsoil, C Weathered rock, D Bedrock`, leaching).
- `p6-agr-m2`: Module 2: Soil Erosion Types & Conservation Methods (`12 questions`: splash, sheet, rill, and gully erosion; conservation via terracing steep hillsides `Kabale/Kapchorwa`, mulching, contour plowing across slopes, strip cropping, cover cropping, check dams `gabions`, rotational grazing, crop rotation, and green manure).
- `p6-agr-m3`: Module 3: Cattle Management & Poultry Farming (`12 questions`: indigenous Ankole long-horn and Zebu resistant to ticks/heat vs exotic Friesian for milk and Aberdeen Angus for beef; rotational grazing `paddocking` and zero grazing `stall feeding inside roofed sheds`; weekly acaricide dipping against East Coast Fever `ECF`; poultry layers vs broilers, Newcastle disease `greenish diarrhea and stargazing neck twisting`, coccidiosis `bloody diarrhea inside damp brooders`, brooders, deep litter, debeaking, incubation).
- `p6-agr-m4`: Module 4: Bee Keeping (Apiculture) & Honey Harvesting (`13 questions`: Queen bee laying eggs and queen substance, Drone fertile males mating during nuptial flight, Worker infertile females performing 100% of hive labor `foraging nectar/pollen, nursing larvae, building wax combs, guarding entrance`; Kenya Top Bar KTB hive vs log hive; smoker, protective overall, extracting ripe capped honeycombs, waggle dance).
