## Master Topic Breakdown & Module Inventory

### 1. Punctuation, Vocabulary, Spelling & Affixes (`p4-eng-grammar-vocab` - 4 Modules, 48 Questions)
- **Module 1: Punctuation Marks & Capitalization** (`p4-eng-vocab-m1`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers capital letters (`proper nouns: Kintu, Jinja, Friday, pronoun 'I', first word of sentences`), full stops (`.`), question marks (`?`), exclamation marks (`!`), commas (`separating shopping list items from Nakasero with 'and' before final item, date separation`), apostrophe of possession (`singular: Akello's uniform, plural ending in s: boys' dormitories vs irregular plurals: children's shoes`), contractions (`didn't, let's, can't`), and direct speech quotation marks (`enclosing exact spoken words`).
- **Module 2: Nouns (Common, Proper, Collective, Abstract & Plurals)** (`p4-eng-vocab-m2`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers Common vs Proper nouns (`market vs Owino Market`), Collective nouns (`a swarm of bees, a herd of cattle, a flock of sheep, a fleet of buses`), Abstract nouns (`kindness, honesty, bravery, wisdom`), Plural formation rules (`regular -s, -es after o: potato -> potatoes, f/fe to -ves: leaf -> leaves, y to -ies after consonant: baby -> babies vs boys after vowel, irregulars: child -> children, ox -> oxen, man -> men, foot -> feet, tooth -> teeth`), Uncountable nouns (`water, sugar, advice, luggage`), invariant nouns (`sheep, furniture`), and compound nouns (`sisters-in-law`).
- **Module 3: Synonyms, Antonyms, Homophones & Gender** (`p4-eng-vocab-m3`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers Synonyms (`begin/start, small/little, rich/wealthy, glad/happy, help/assist`), Antonyms (`polite/rude, brave/cowardly, careful/careless via -ful/-less opposite suffixes`), Homophones (`their/there/they're, to/two/too, hear/here, weak/week, son/sun, right/write`), and Gender (`King/Queen, prince/princess, uncle/aunt, nephew/niece, actor/actress via -ess, Bull/Cow, Ram/Ewe, Boar/Sow, Cock/Hen`).
- **Module 4: Spelling, Abbreviations, Prefixes & Suffixes** (`p4-eng-vocab-m4`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers negative prefixes (`un- happy, dis- agree, im- polite, mis- behave`), noun/adjective suffixes (`-er teacher, -ness kindness, -able comfortable, -ful beautiful with ONE 'l' dropped`), standard abbreviations (`Mr., Mrs., Dr., P.O., e.g. exempli gratia, i.e. id est, a.m./p.m.`), dropping silent 'e' before '-ing' (`write -> writing`), changing 'y' to 'i' before '-ful' (`beautiful`), and spelling traps (`grammar vs grammer, library vs libary, necessary, Wednesday, February`).

### 2. Comprehensive English Grammar & Sentence Structures (`p4-eng-grammar-structures` - 5 Modules, 60 Questions)
- **Module 1: Verbs & Simple Tenses (Present Simple, Past Simple & Future Simple)** (`p4-eng-gram-m1`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers Present Simple tense (`habitual routines & universal truths: Kintu irons uniform, sun rises in East, third-person singular -s/-es vs plural base verb`), Past Simple tense (`regular C-V-C doubling: clap -> clapped, irregular past tenses: buy -> bought, catch -> caught, teach -> taught, go -> went, eat -> ate, write -> wrote, speak -> spoke`), negative past simple (`did not + base verb: did not see`), and Future Simple auxiliary verbs (`shall for first person I/we vs will`).
- **Module 2: Continuous Tenses & Subject-Verb Agreement** (`p4-eng-gram-m2`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers Present Continuous (`is/am/are + verb-ing: pupils are reading right now, C-V-C doubling run -> running`), Past Continuous (`was/were + verb-ing: Mukasa was digging when rain started`), Subject-Verb Agreement (`singular subject -> singular verb: dog barks, plural subject -> plural verb: dogs bark, separated prepositional phrases: A basket of ripe mangoes IS, neither...nor / either...or taking singular verb: Neither Kintu nor his brother IS, pairs of items: A pair IS vs Two pairs ARE`).
- **Module 3: Pronouns & Adjectives (Types, Comparison & Order)** (`p4-eng-gram-m3`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers Personal pronouns (`subject position: Kintu and Akello -> They vs object: them`), Possessive pronouns (`his, hers, mine, yours, ours, theirs without apostrophe`), Reflexive pronouns (`myself, yourself, himself, herself, ourselves, themselves matching subject`), Adjectives and Degrees of Comparison (`Positive, Comparative -er/more, Superlative -est/most: heavy -> heavier, tall -> the tallest, long multi-syllable more/most interesting, double comparative error 'more taller', irregular comparison: good -> better -> best, bad -> worse -> worst, many -> more -> most, little -> less -> least, equal comparison as...as`), and Order of Adjectives (`OSASCOM: Opinion, Size, Age, Shape, Color, Origin, Material: a lovely small new round brown wooden Ugandan dining table`).
- **Module 4: Adverbs, Prepositions & Articles** (`p4-eng-gram-m4`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers Adverbs of Manner (`-ly: neatly, quickly telling HOW`), sequence of adverbs (`M-P-T: Manner -> Place -> Time`), Prepositions of time (`at 8:00, on Friday, in June, for 10 years duration vs since 2016`), prepositions of spatial movement (`over the fence, into the classroom, off the table`), between vs among (`between two items vs among three or more items`), fixed preposition collocations (`fond OF, interested IN, good AT, married TO, cut WITH a saw vs cut BY a carpenter`), and Articles (`definite 'the' for unique bodies: the sky, the sun, the River Nile vs indefinite 'a/an' governed strictly by spoken initial sound: an umbrella, an hour, an honest boy vs a university, a European`).
- **Module 5: Conjunctions & Sentence Joining Structures** (`p4-eng-gram-m5`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers Coordinating conjunctions (`and adding, but contrasting, or choice, because showing cause/reason`), subordinating time conjunctions (`when, as soon as requiring comma if at start of sentence`), correlative conjunction pairs (`Both...and... making subject plural: Both Akello and Babirye ARE, Either...or..., Neither...nor..., Prefer...to... NOT than`), and sentence joining structures (`...too...to... negative excessive: too heavy to lift, ...so...that... cause and effect with full result clause: so hot that Akello could not drink it, ...enough to... placed AFTER adjective: tall enough to touch, Although/Even though concession, Unless meaning if not without double negatives`).

### 3. Reading Comprehension & Critical Analysis (`p4-eng-comprehension` - 3 Modules, 36 Questions)
- **Module 1: Interpreting Notices, Announcements, Posters & Timetables** (`p4-eng-comp-m1`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers analyzing authentic school notices (`St. Jude Primary School PTA meeting on 25th July at 9:00 a.m. identifying target audience and agenda`), interpreting class timetables (`calculating 30-minute break duration from 10:00 to 10:30 AM`), public health and safety posters (`Stop Cholera handwashing with soap, Road safety 'Speed Kills - Arrive Alive', high voltage electricity transformer warning signs`), Lost and Found notices (`specific descriptions, date/place lost, owner contact, and polite reward promises`), formal circular reading order (`Header -> Audience/Date -> Subject/Body -> Official closing/Headteacher signature`), and strict deadlines (`Not later than due dates`).
- **Module 2: Reading Dialogues, Short Stories & Fables** (`p4-eng-comp-m2`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers reading grocery market dialogues (`Owino Market transaction: calculating balance/change of UGX 2,000 when paying UGX 10,000 for 2 kg of yellow beans at UGX 4,000/kg and identifying iron-rich Nambale beans at UGX 4,500/kg`), analyzing moral fables (`personification/anthropomorphism of animals: Aesop's fable of the Lion and the Mouse teaching 'Little friends may prove great friends', identifying incisor teeth used to chew ropes`), understanding context clues for unfamiliar vocabulary, reading factual travelogues (`Budo Primary School game drive to Queen Elizabeth National Park in Kasese observing Uganda kobs, African elephants, and explaining why lions climb Mituba fig trees to escape biting tsetse flies and enjoy valley breezes`), chronological sequence of events, matching characters to roles, and recognizing tone (`polite respect vs desperate fear`).
- **Module 3: Interpreting Calendars, Charts, Graphs & Pictographs** (`p4-eng-comp-m3`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers interpreting weekly attendance registers (`identifying Wednesday's lowest attendance of 45 present / 5 absent due to heavy morning rain`), calendar rhymes and month durations (`identifying June as a 30-day month along with Sept, April, Nov; December as 12th month ending on New Year's Eve 31st Dec; leap years with 29-day Feb`), calculating durations in weeks/days (`Monday 6th July to Monday 27th July = 21 days = 3 weeks`), national holidays (`Uganda Martyrs' Day on June 3rd at Namugongo Shrine, Independence Day on October 9th`), reading pictographs (`using the legend where 1 book symbol = 2 storybooks to calculate Akello's 5 symbols as 10 books read`), interpreting bar graphs and axes (`X-axis and Y-axis labels, summing rainfall across May 15 days + June 10 days + July 5 days = 30 total rainy days`), and understanding why term calendars are published (`transparent planning for exams and events`).

### 4. Composition, Formal Letter Writing & Functional Writing (`p4-eng-composition` - 4 Modules, 48 Questions)
- **Module 1: Guided & Free Composition Writing** (`p4-eng-comp-w1`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers paragraph structure (`Topic sentence stating central idea at start vs detailed library topic sentence, supporting details, Concluding sentence wrapping up with satisfying finish or moral`), paragraph indentation and block double-spacing, sequence connectors (`First, Next, Then, After that, Finally at climax`), re-arranging jumbled sentences (`logical and chronological flow of a school clean-up day: 2 -> 4 -> 3 -> 1`), rich descriptive vocabulary, neat handwriting (`canceling mistakes with single horizontal ruler line`), the 4-stage writing process (`Pre-writing/Brainstorming -> Drafting -> Revising/Editing -> Proofreading & Final Copying`), transition words (`However, Furthermore, Because, In conclusion`), and Picture compositions (`studying all 6 picture boxes sequentially before writing`).
- **Module 2: Informal / Friendly Letter Writing** (`p4-eng-comp-w2`) - 12 Questions

- **Module 4: Filling Forms, Writing Messages & Functional Documents** (`p4-eng-comp-w4`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers filling biographical data on official forms (`Surname / Family clan name like Okello vs Given personal names; D.O.B. meaning Date of Birth to determine exact age bracket; Gender / Sex checking [ ] MALE or [ ] FEMALE boxes; Nationality as UGANDAN for citizens of Uganda`), spatial discipline (`writing signatures and block letters strictly inside form box borders without overlapping onto barcodes for digital scanning`), declaration statements (`legally binding applicant to truth and preventing forged certificates`), the 4-step form filling procedure (`reading entire blank form first before writing block letters`), telephone message slips (`taking exact time, date, caller contact, and message to ensure reliable communication without misquoting`), parental excuse notes (`model apology note with exact dates, medical reasons from Mengo Hospital, and parent phone contact for verification`), and functional writing definition (`practical literacy for everyday administrative tasks`).
