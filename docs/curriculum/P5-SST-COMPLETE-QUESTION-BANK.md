## Complete Curriculum Structure (`8 Master Topics -> 25 Modules -> 303 Questions`)

### 1. Location, Size & Boundaries of Uganda (`p5-sst-location`) - 3 Modules, 36 Questions
- **Module 1: Latitudes, Longitudes, Equator & Time Calculations** (`p5-location-m1`, 12 Questions)
  - Latitudes ($1^\circ\text{S}$ to $4^\circ\text{N}$) and Longitudes ($29^\circ\text{E}$ to $35^\circ\text{E}$); Equator crossing Uganda (`Entebbe, Kayabwe, Kasese`); Prime Meridian (`Greenwich $0^\circ$`) and time calculations ($4\text{ min/degree}$, East Africa Time $\text{GMT}+3$).
- **Module 2: Uganda as a Landlocked Country & Regional Access Routes** (`p5-location-m2`, 12 Questions)
  - Definition of landlocked; Northern Corridor via Port of Mombasa (`Kenya`) and Central Corridor via Port of Dar es Salaam (`Tanzania`); Inland dry ports (`Port Bell Luzira, Nakawa`); One-Stop Border Posts (`OSBP` at Busia, Malaba, Mutukula); UNCLOS sea access rights; Smuggling and URA border enforcement.
- **Module 3: Neighbors, Boundaries, Regions & Administrative Districts** (`p5-location-m3`, 12 Questions)
  - Neighbors (`North: South Sudan; East: Kenya; South: Tanzania & Rwanda; West: DR Congo 870 km boundary`); Natural vs artificial boundaries; 4 Geographical regions (`Northern largest area, Central, Eastern, Western`); Total surface area ($\approx 241,038\text{ km}^2$, $18\%$ water/swamps); Decentralization and LC5 District/Municipality administration.

### 2. Physical Features of Uganda (`p5-sst-physical-features`) - 3 Modules, 37 Questions
- **Module 1: Mountains & Highlands (Mt. Rwenzori, Mt. Elgon, Mufumbiro)** (`p5-physical-m1`, 12 Questions)
  - Horst / Block mountains (`Mt. Rwenzori 5,109m Margherita Peak`); Volcanic mountains (`Mt. Elgon 4,321m Wagagai Peak, Mt. Mufumbiro/Virunga in SW Kisoro, Mt. Moroto`); Residual hills (`Inselbergs like Tororo Rock`); Normal lapse rate ($6.5^\circ\text{C per } 1,000\text{m}$); Bench terracing in Kigezi (`Kabale`) and Imbalu cultural traditions on Mt. Elgon.
- **Module 2: Drainage Systems: Lakes & Rivers (Lake Victoria, River Nile)** (`p5-physical-m2`, 13 Questions)
  - Basin / depression lakes (`Lake Victoria 68,800 km^2 shared 45% UG, Lake Kyoga shallowest/papyrus floating islands Sudd`); Rift valley lakes (`Lake Albert Lutanze, Lake Edward Rutanzige, Lake George connected by 32-km Kazinga Channel hippos`); Crater lakes (`Lake Katwe salt panning`) and lava-dammed lakes (`Lake Bunyonyi 29 islands, Bilharzia-free`); River Nile (`6,695 km, Victoria Nile -> Lake Kyoga -> Murchison Falls 43m drop -> Albert Nile -> South Sudan, hydroelectric dams Nalubaale, Kiira, Bujagali, Isimba, Karuma 600 MW`); River Katonga bifurcated drainage.
- **Module 3: Rift Valley, Plateaus & Wetland/Swamp Conservation** (`p5-physical-m3`, 12 Questions)
  - Western (`Albertine`) Rift Valley (`faulting, Butiaba/Kyambura escarpments, crude oil deposits`); Central African Plateau ($900\text{m}-1,500\text{m}$ elevation moderating climate); Wetlands (`Mabamba, Lubigi`) acting as natural sponges and water filters; NEMA wetland protection vs illegal encroachment/drainage; Papyrus (`Cyperus papyrus`) craft utilization; Rain shadow on rift valley floor; Ramsar Convention (`1971`) 12 protected wetland sites.

### 3. Climate & Natural Vegetation of Uganda (`p5-sst-climate-vegetation`) - 3 Modules, 36 Questions
- **Module 1: Weather vs Climate, Elements & Meteorological Instruments** (`p5-climate-m1`, 12 Questions)
  - Weather (`daily atmospheric condition`) vs Climate (`30-35 year average`); Weather instruments (`Rain gauge mm at 09:00 hrs, Hygrometer relative humidity %, Wind vane direction, Anemometer speed km/h, Barometer pressure mb, Campbell-Stokes sunshine recorder hours`); Stevenson Screen (`louvred white wooden box $1.2\text{m}$ high facing south/away from sun`); Diurnal temperature range; Convectional vs relief rainfall.
- **Module 2: Climate Zones of Uganda (Equatorial, Savannah, Montane, Semi-Arid)** (`p5-climate-m2`, 12 Questions)
  - Tropical Savannah (`60% of UG, wet/dry seasons, $800-1,200\text{mm}$ rain, cereal farming`); Equatorial climate (`double maxima rain $>1,500\text{mm}$ around Lake Victoria/rainforests, Robusta coffee/matooke`); Semi-Arid Karamoja (`$<500\text{mm}$ unreliable rain, nomadic pastoralism Zebu cattle`); Montane climate (`high altitude cooling, relief rain, Arabica coffee/potatoes`); ITCZ migration; Land/Lake breezes; Deforestation vs afforestation/re-afforestation/agroforestry; El Niño vs La Niña.
- **Module 3: Natural Vegetation Types & Altitude Zoning** (`p5-climate-m3`, 12 Questions)
  - Natural vs artificial vegetation; Tropical Rainforests (`Mabira, Budongo, Bugoma - emergent canopy, buttress roots, lianas/creepers, mahogany/mvule`); Tropical Savannah Grasslands (`acacia umbrella tops/thorns, elephant grass, baobab water storage, pyrophytes`); Deciduous drought shedding; Altitude zoning on Mt. Rwenzori (`Savannah base -> Rainforest -> Bamboo forest gorillas -> Heath & Moorland/Alpine giant lobelias -> Bare rock/snow peak`); Eucalyptus (`Kalitunsi`) swamp drainage vs water table depletion; NFA forest reserve management.

### 4. The People & Pre-Colonial History of Uganda (`p5-sst-people-history`) - 3 Modules, 36 Questions
- **Module 1: Original Inhabitants & Bantu Migrations & Kingdoms** (`p5-history-m1`, 12 Questions)
  - Stone-age Batwa (`Bambuti pygmies hunter-gatherers`); Bantu migration from Congo Basin (`1000-1500 AD, iron working, agriculture, over 60% population: Baganda, Banyankole, Banyoro, Basoga, Batoro, Bakiga, Bagisu`); Centralized Kingdoms: Bunyoro-Kitara (`Bachwezi cattle trenches Bigo bya Mugenyi, followed by Babito dynasty Isingoma Rukidi Mpuga`), Buganda (`Kabaka at Mengo palace, Katikkiro, Lukiiko, Saza/Gombolola/Miruka pyramid`), Toro (`Prince Kaboyo breakaway 1830`), Ankole (`Omugabe, sacred drums Bagyendanwa, Bahima cattle/Bairu farmers`); Mutuba bark-cloth tree (`Olubugo`).
- **Module 2: Nilotic & Sudanic Migrations (River-Lake Luo, Karamojong, Sabiny)** (`p5-history-m2`, 12 Questions)
  - River-Lake Nilotes (`Luo from Bahr-el-Ghazal South Sudan, split at Pubungu/Pakwach over Gipir and Labongo spear/bead dispute -> Acholi, Alur, Japadhola`); Plains Nilotes (`Nilo-Hamites from Ethiopian highlands -> Karamojong pastoralists, Iteso mixed farmers in Teso, Kakwa, Kumam blended tribe`); Highland Nilotes (`Sabiny/Sebei on Mt. Elgon slopes`); Sudanic speakers (`Lugbara, Madi, Kakwa in West Nile`); Migration push factors (`prolonged drought, rinderpest, sleeping sickness, inter-clan warfare`).
- **Module 3: Pre-Colonial Segmentary Societies, Customs, Iron Smelting & Trade** (`p5-history-m3`, 12 Questions)
  - Decentralized / segmentary societies (`Acholi Rwot, Iteso Won Nyaci, Bakiga - Council of Elders democratically adjudicating justice via restitution`); Omukago (`Blood brotherhood covenant mixing blood with coffee berry`); Barter trade (`double coincidence of wants`); Lake Katwe and Kibiro salt mining (`white gold`); Blacksmithing (`smelting iron ore in clay furnaces with Emyuba bellows producing Enfuka hoes and spears`); Totem (`Omuziro`) conservation system; Traditional informal fireplace (`Ekyoto`) education; Cowrie shells (`Ensimbi`) currency; Indigenous herbal medicine (`NCRI`).

### 5. Foreign Influence & Colonization of Uganda (`p5-sst-foreign-influence`) - 4 Modules, 49 Questions
- **Module 1: Arab & Persian Traders (Slave/Ivory Trade, Islam, Kiswahili)** (`p5-foreign-m1`, 12 Questions)
  - Ahmed bin Ibrahim reaching Mengo (`1844`) under Kabaka Suuna II; Long-distance dhow/caravan trade from Zanzibar (`Sultan Seyyid Said`) via Tabora to Buganda exchanging Amerikani cloth, guns, and cowries for Elephant Ivory and Slaves (`Tippu Tip`); Introduction of Islam under Kabaka Mutesa I; Kiswahili lingua franca; Kanzu and Gomesi (`Busuti`) national attire; Introduction of firearms enabling Kabalega (`Abarusura army`) and Mutesa I to dominate neighbors.
- **Module 2: European Explorers & Christian Missionaries (Speke, Baker, Martyrs)** (`p5-foreign-m2`, 13 Questions)
  - Explorers: John Hanning Speke (`Ripon Falls 1862`), Samuel & Florence Baker (`Lake Albert/Murchison Falls 1864`), Henry Morton Stanley (`circumnavigated Lake Victoria 1875, wrote Daily Telegraph letter inviting missionaries`); Missionaries: CMS Anglicans (`June 1877, Alexander Mackay printing press/carpentry Abasomi`), Catholic White Fathers (`Feb 1879, Father Lourdel Mapeera and Brother Amans`), Dr. Albert Cook (`Mengo Hospital 1897, identified sleeping sickness`); Elite schools (`Namilyango 1902, Gayaza 1905, Budo/SMACK 1906`); Kabaka Mwanga II executing 45 Christian Martyrs at Namugongo (`1885-1887, chief executioner Mukajanga, 3rd June Martyrs' Day`) and murder of Bishop Hannington in Busoga (`1885 back door eastern route taboo`); Kenneth Borup introducing cotton (`1903`).
- **Module 3: British Colonization, IBEAC & 1900 Buganda Agreement** (`p5-foreign-m3`, 12 Questions)
  - 1890 Heligoland Anglo-German Treaty; Imperial British East Africa Company (`IBEAC under Captain Lugard, Fort Kampala 1890, Sudanese troops, financial bankruptcy 1892`); Sir Gerald Portal raising Union Jack (`1st April 1893`) and declaring Protectorate (`18th June 1894`); The 1900 Buganda Agreement (`signed by Sir Harry Johnston and Regents Sir Apolo Kaggwa, Zakaria Kisingiri, Stanslas Mugwanya for infant Kabaka Daudi Chwa II - introduced Mailo private land 8,000 sq miles, Lukiiko 89 members, 20 Saza counties including Buyaga/Bugangaizi Lost Counties, Hut/Gun taxes 3 Shillings`); Indirect Rule (`governing via native African kings/chiefs and DCs`); Peasant agricultural economy policy by Governor Hesketh Bell (`avoiding white settler land seizures`); Entebbe vs Kampala capital; Uganda Railway (`Lunatic Express 1896-1901 Mombasa to Kisumu/Kampala, 32,000 Indian coolies/Allidina Visram dukas`).
- **Module 4: Colonial Expansion & Resistance (Kabalega, Mwanga, Lamogi)** (`p5-foreign-m4`, 12 Questions)
  - Semei Kakungulu (`Muganda general conquering Eastern Uganda Busoga/Teso/Mbale Mvule trees, Abayudaya Jewish community`); Omukama Chwa II Kabalega of Bunyoro (`6-year guerrilla war with Abarusura army, captured in Lango 1899 alongside Kabaka Mwanga II, exiled to Seychelles islands, Mwanga died 1903, Kabalega returned 1923`); Lamogi Guru-Guru caves rebellion (`1911 under Chief Awich of Payira against disarmament/Gun tax`); Nyangire Rebellion in Bunyoro (`1907 peaceful civil boycott rejecting Baganda Saza chiefs imposed by Indirect Rule`); 1897 Sudanese Nubian Mutiny (`Fort Lubwa Jinja`); Early higher institutions (`Makerere Technical College 1922`); Kasanvu forced public labor (`30 days unpaid until abolished 1922`); Cash crop double-loop (`cotton/coffee paying taxes and feeding UK factories`); Indian Rupee and East African Shilling (`1921 EACB`).
