## 1. P5 Mathematics: Fractions, Decimals & LCM/GCF (`p5-math-fractions`)

### Question 1 (`multiple_choice`)
- **Question**: What is the sum of $\frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{6}$ expressed in simplest form?
- **Options**:
  - A. $\frac{2}{9}$ (`false`)
  - B. $\frac{1}{2}$ (`true`)
  - C. $\frac{3}{6}$ (`false`)
  - D. $\frac{2}{6}$ (`false`)
- **Hint**: First find the Least Common Multiple (LCM) of 3 and 6, which is 6.
- **Explanation**: $\frac{1}{3} = \frac{2}{6}$. Then $\frac{2}{6} + \frac{1}{6} = \frac{3}{6}$. Simplifying by dividing top and bottom by 3 gives $\frac{1}{2}$.
- **Deep Dive**: Always find a common denominator before adding or subtracting fractions with unlike denominators.

### Question 2 (`short_answer`)
- **Question**: A trader in Owino Market bought a bag of Irish potatoes for **UGX 45,000** and sold it for **UGX 54,000**. How much **profit** in UGX did the trader make?
- **Answer**: 9000
- **Hint**: $\text{Profit} = \text{Selling Price} - \text{Cost Price}$.
- **Explanation**: $54,000 - 45,000 = \text{UGX } 9,000$.
- **Deep Dive**: When the Selling Price (SP) is greater than the Cost Price (CP), a profit is made. If the Cost Price is greater than the Selling Price, a loss is incurred ($\text{Loss} = \text{CP} - \text{SP}$).

### Question 3 (`true_false`)
- **Question**: In Roman numerals, the symbol **L** represents the value **50** and **C** represents **100**.
- **Answer**: `true`
- **Hint**: Think of the sequence: I=1, V=5, X=10, L=50, C=100, D=500, M=1000.
- **Explanation**: L = 50 and C = 100. For example, XC = $100 - 10 = 90$, and LX = $50 + 10 = 60$.

### Question 4 (`ordering`)
- **Question**: Arrange these decimal values from **smallest to largest**.
- **Items**:
  - A. 0.05 (`id: d1`)
  - B. 0.5 (`id: d2`)
  - C. 0.15 (`id: d3`)
  - D. 0.01 (`id: d4`)
- **Correct Order**: `["d4", "d1", "d3", "d2"]` (0.01, 0.05, 0.15, 0.5)
- **Explanation**: Look at the tenths place first: 0.01 and 0.05 have 0 tenths (so check hundredths: 1 < 5). Then 0.15 has 1 tenth, and 0.5 has 5 tenths (0.50).

## 2. P5 Social Studies: Regions of Uganda & Physical Features (`p5-sst-regions`)

### Question 1 (`multiple_choice`)
- **Question**: Which is the **highest mountain** in Uganda?
- **Options**:
  - A. Mount Elgon (`false`)
  - B. Mount Rwenzori (`true`)
  - C. Mount Moroto (`false`)
  - D. Mount Mufumbiro (`false`)
- **Hint**: It is block mountain located on the border between Uganda and DR Congo with snow-capped Margherita peak.
- **Explanation**: Mount Rwenzori is Uganda's highest mountain at 5,109 meters above sea level (Margherita Peak).
- **Deep Dive**: Unlike Mount Elgon and Mount Moroto which are volcanic mountains formed by eruption, Mount Rwenzori is a block (or horst) mountain formed by faulting and uplifting during the formation of the Western Rift Valley.

### Question 2 (`short_answer`)
- **Question**: Name the largest lake in Uganda and East Africa which is shared with Kenya and Tanzania.
- **Answer**: Lake Victoria
- **Hint**: It is a shallow basin lake formed by crustal down-warping.
- **Explanation**: Lake Victoria is the largest lake in East Africa and the second-largest freshwater lake in the world.
- **Deep Dive**: Lake Victoria is a major source of tilapia and Nile perch, and acts as the source of the River Nile at Jinja.

### Question 3 (`true_false`)
- **Question**: The equator ($0^\circ$ latitude) crosses Uganda near Kayabwe on the Kampala-Masaka highway.
- **Answer**: `true`
- **Hint**: Kayabwe is famous for the circular equator monument where water experiments are performed for tourists.
- **Explanation**: Uganda lies astride the equator, which divides the country into the Northern and Southern hemispheres.

## 3. P5 Integrated Science: The Human Body & Tropical Sanitation (`p5-sci-humanbody`)

### Question 1 (`multiple_choice`)
- **Question**: Which insect vector spreads **Malaria** in human beings?
- **Options**:
  - A. Housefly (`false`)
  - B. Tsetse fly (`false`)
  - C. Female Anopheles mosquito (`true`)
  - D. Culex mosquito (`false`)
- **Hint**: Only the female species bites to get blood protein for laying eggs.
- **Explanation**: The female Anopheles mosquito transmits the *Plasmodium* parasite that causes malaria.
- **Deep Dive**: Malaria can be prevented by sleeping under insecticide-treated mosquito nets (ITNs), draining stagnant water where mosquitoes breed, clearing bushes near homes, and spraying insecticides.

### Question 2 (`short_answer`)
- **Question**: How many bones make up the adult human skeleton?
- **Answer**: 206
- **Hint**: Babies are born with about 300 bones that fuse together as they grow.
- **Explanation**: An adult human skeleton consists of 206 bones that provide support, movement, and protection.

### Question 3 (`matching`)
- **Question**: Match each disease-carrying insect vector to the tropical disease it transmits in Uganda.
- **Pairs**:
  - `v1`: **Housefly** $\rightarrow$ Cholera, Typhoid and Dysentery (via contaminated food/water)
  - `v2`: **Tsetse fly** $\rightarrow$ Sleeping sickness (African Trypanosomiasis)
  - `v3`: **Aedes mosquito** $\rightarrow$ Yellow fever and Dengue fever
  - `v4`: **Water snail** $\rightarrow$ Bilharzia (Schistosomiasis) in lakes and swamps
- **Explanation**: Controlling vector populations is the most effective way to eliminate tropical diseases across Ugandan communities.

## 4. P5 English Language: Verbs, Tenses & Vocabulary (`p5-eng-tenses`)

### Question 1 (`multiple_choice`)
- **Question**: Choose the correct word to complete the sentence: "Neither Amina nor her brothers ______ at school when the rain started."
- **Options**:
  - A. was (`false`)
  - B. were (`true`)
  - C. is (`false`)
  - D. has been (`false`)
- **Hint**: When using *neither... nor*, the verb agrees with the subject closest to the verb ('brothers' = plural).
- **Explanation**: Because 'brothers' is plural and comes right before the verb, we use plural past tense 'were'.

### Question 2 (`short_answer`)
- **Question**: What is the **synonym** (word with similar meaning) of the word **Purchase** when buying items in a market?
- **Answer**: Buy
- **Hint**: It starts with 'B'.
- **Explanation**: To purchase means to buy goods or services using money.

### Question 3 (`true_false`)
- **Question**: An adverb is a word that modifies or tells us more about a verb, adjective, or another adverb.
- **Answer**: `true`
- **Hint**: Many adverbs end in **-ly** (e.g., *quickly, neatly, loudly*).
- **Explanation**: For example, in "The pupil ran quickly," 'quickly' is an adverb telling us *how* the action 'ran' happened.
