## 1. P6 Mathematics: Percentages, Ratios & Set Theory (`p6-math-percentages`)

### Question 1 (`multiple_choice`)
- **Question**: In a P6 class of **50 pupils**, **20%** of the pupils are absent due to heavy rain. How many pupils are **present**?
- **Options**:
  - A. 10 pupils (`false`)
  - B. 40 pupils (`true`)
  - C. 30 pupils (`false`)
  - D. 20 pupils (`false`)
- **Hint**: First find the number of absent pupils ($20\% \text{ of } 50$), then subtract from 50.
- **Explanation**: $\frac{20}{100} \times 50 = 10\text{ absent pupils}$. Therefore, $50 - 10 = 40\text{ pupils present}$. (Alternatively: $80\% \text{ present} = \frac{80}{100} \times 50 = 40$).

### Question 2 (`short_answer`)
- **Question**: If Set $A = \{2, 3, 5, 7\}$ and Set $B = \{1, 3, 5, 9\}$, find the number of elements in the intersection set $n(A \cap B)$.
- **Answer**: 2
- **Hint**: Look for the elements that belong to both Set A and Set B.
- **Explanation**: The intersection set $A \cap B = \{3, 5\}$. There are 2 common elements, so $n(A \cap B) = 2$.

### Question 3 (`true_false`)
- **Question**: If $3x = 24$, then the value of variable $x$ is $8$.
- **Answer**: `true`
- **Hint**: Divide both sides of the equation by 3.
- **Explanation**: $\frac{3x}{3} = \frac{24}{3}$, giving $x = 8$.

## 2. P6 Social Studies: East African Community & Early Kingdoms (`p6-sst-eac`)

### Question 1 (`multiple_choice`)
- **Question**: Which of the following countries is **NOT** a member state of the East African Community (EAC)?
- **Options**:
  - A. Republic of South Sudan (`false`)
  - B. Democratic Republic of Congo (`false`)
  - C. Federal Democratic Republic of Ethiopia (`true`)
  - D. Republic of Rwanda (`false`)
- **Hint**: Ethiopia lies in the Horn of Africa and has not joined the EAC block.
- **Explanation**: The 8 EAC partner states are Uganda, Kenya, Tanzania, Rwanda, Burundi, South Sudan, DR Congo, and Somalia. Ethiopia is not an EAC member.

### Question 2 (`short_answer`)
- **Question**: Name the city where the headquarters of the East African Community (EAC Secretariat) are located.
- **Answer**: Arusha
- **Hint**: It is a major northern city in Tanzania near Mount Meru.
- **Explanation**: Arusha, Tanzania, serves as the diplomatic and administrative headquarters of the EAC.

### Question 3 (`true_false`)
- **Question**: The Bunyoro-Kitara Empire was one of the earliest and largest pre-colonial kingdoms in East Africa, founded by the Bachwezi.
- **Answer**: `true`
- **Hint**: The Bachwezi introduced long-horned cattle, iron smelting, and regalia.
- **Explanation**: Bunyoro-Kitara covered large parts of Western Uganda and was famous for its cattlekeeping and iron working under the Chwezi dynasty.

## 3. P6 Integrated Science: Circulatory System & Electricity (`p6-sci-electricity`)

### Question 1 (`multiple_choice`)
- **Question**: Which blood vessels carry oxygenated blood **away from the heart** to the rest of the body?
- **Options**:
  - A. Veins (`false`)
  - B. Capillaries (`false`)
  - C. Arteries (`true`)
  - D. Vena Cava (`false`)
- **Hint**: Remember 'A' for Arteries = 'A' for Away!
- **Explanation**: Arteries carry blood away from the heart under high pressure. All arteries carry oxygenated blood except the pulmonary artery.

### Question 2 (`short_answer`)
- **Question**: Which blood cells are responsible for fighting against disease germs that enter the human body?
- **Answer**: White blood cells
- **Hint**: They act as the body's internal army and soldiers.
- **Explanation**: White blood cells (leucocytes) engulf and destroy pathogens or produce antibodies to fight infection.

### Question 3 (`true_false`)
- **Question**: Copper wire is a good conductor of electricity, while rubber and dry wood are electric insulators.
- **Answer**: `true`
- **Hint**: Conductors allow electric current to flow through them easily.
- **Explanation**: Metals like copper, aluminum, and silver conduct electricity, while non-metals like rubber and wood block electric current and prevent electrocution.

## 4. P6 English Language: Direct & Reported Speech (`p6-eng-clauses`)

### Question 1 (`multiple_choice`)
- **Question**: Choose the correct **reported speech** form of: *Okello said, "I am reading my English notes now."*
- **Options**:
  - A. Okello said that he is reading his English notes now. (`false`)
  - B. Okello said that he was reading his English notes then. (`true`)
  - C. Okello said that I was reading my notes then. (`false`)
  - D. Okello says he read his English notes. (`false`)
- **Hint**: Present continuous (*am reading*) changes to past continuous (*was reading*), and *now* changes to *then*.
- **Explanation**: In reported speech, verbs backshift in tense and time words change (*now* $\rightarrow$ *then*, *today* $\rightarrow$ *that day*).

### Question 2 (`short_answer`)
- **Question**: Complete the sentence with the correct preposition: "All pupils should pay attention ______ their teacher during mathematics drills."
- **Answer**: to
- **Hint**: 'Pay attention' is always followed by this two-letter preposition.
- **Explanation**: The correct phrasal preposition is *pay attention to*.
