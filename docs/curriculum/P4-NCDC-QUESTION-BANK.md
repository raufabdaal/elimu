## 1. P4 Mathematics: Whole Numbers, Basic Operations & Simple Fractions (`p4-math-numbers`)

### Question 1 (`multiple_choice`)
- **Question**: What is the place value of digit **6** in the number **4,682**?
- **Options**:
  - A. Tens (`false`)
  - B. Hundreds (`true`)
  - C. Thousands (`false`)
  - D. Ones (`false`)
- **Hint**: Look from right to left: Ones (2), Tens (8), Hundreds (6), Thousands (4).
- **Explanation**: In 4,682, the digit 6 is in the Hundreds place.
- **Deep Dive**: Every digit in a whole number has a place value. 4 is in the thousands place ($4 \times 1,000 = 4,000$), 6 is in the hundreds place ($6 \times 100 = 600$), 8 is in the tens place ($8 \times 10 = 80$), and 2 is in the ones place ($2 \times 1 = 2$).

### Question 2 (`short_answer`)
- **Question**: Kintu went to the local market and bought 3 exercise books at **UGX 1,200** each. How much money did he pay altogether?
- **Answer**: 3600
- **Hint**: Multiply 1,200 by 3.
- **Explanation**: $1,200 \times 3 = \text{UGX } 3,600$.
- **Deep Dive**: When finding the total cost of equal items, multiply the cost of one item by the number of items bought.

### Question 3 (`true_false`)
- **Question**: A square is a flat shape with four equal sides and four right angles.
- **Answer**: `true`
- **Hint**: Think about the shape of a checkerboard or a standard floor tile.
- **Explanation**: A square always has 4 sides of equal length ($s$) and four $90^\circ$ right angles.
- **Deep Dive**: The perimeter of a square is found by adding all four sides ($P = 4 \times s$). If one side is 5 cm, the perimeter is $4 \times 5\text{ cm} = 20\text{ cm}$.

### Question 4 (`ordering`)
- **Question**: Arrange these whole numbers from the **smallest to the largest** value.
- **Items**:
  - A. 1,450 (`id: n1`)
  - B. 2,300 (`id: n2`)
  - C. 4,100 (`id: n3`)
  - D. 980 (`id: n4`)
- **Correct Order**: `["n4", "n1", "n2", "n3"]` (980, 1,450, 2,300, 4,100)
- **Explanation**: 980 has only 3 digits (Hundreds), while the others have 4 digits (Thousands). Among the 4-digit numbers, 1,450 < 2,300 < 4,100.

### Question 5 (`matching`)
- **Question**: Match the mathematical shape or term to its correct definition.
- **Pairs**:
  - `p1`: **Rectangle** $\rightarrow$ Flat shape with two pairs of equal opposite sides
  - `p2`: **Perimeter** $\rightarrow$ Total distance around the outside boundary of a shape
  - `p3`: **Numerator** $\rightarrow$ Top number in a fraction showing parts selected
  - `p4`: **Denominator** $\rightarrow$ Bottom number in a fraction showing total equal parts
- **Explanation**: Understanding basic geometric and fraction definitions is foundational in Primary 4 Mathematics.

## 2. P4 Social Studies: Our District & Local Council Leaders (`p4-sst-district`)

### Question 1 (`multiple_choice`)
- **Question**: Who is the political head of a Local Council One (LC1) in a Ugandan village?
- **Options**:
  - A. Chief Administrative Officer (CAO) (`false`)
  - B. LC1 Chairperson (`true`)
  - C. Resident District Commissioner (RDC) (`false`)
  - D. Parish Chief (`false`)
- **Hint**: Every village or cell in Uganda has an LC1 Executive Committee led by a chairperson.
- **Explanation**: The LC1 Chairperson is the elected political leader and head of the village.
- **Deep Dive**: The LC system in Uganda runs from LC1 (Village/Cell), LC2 (Parish/Ward), LC3 (Sub-county/Division), up to LC5 (District/City). The LC1 Chairperson resolves minor community disputes and promotes village security.

### Question 2 (`short_answer`)
- **Question**: Name the instrument that shows the four cardinal points (North, South, East, West) and helps travelers find direction.
- **Answer**: Compass
- **Hint**: It has a magnetic needle that always points North.
- **Explanation**: A compass is a navigational instrument used to find directions on land or sea.
- **Deep Dive**: The four cardinal points are North (N), South (S), East (E), and West (W). The four secondary or intermediate points are North-East (NE), North-West (NW), South-East (SE), and South-West (SW).

### Question 3 (`true_false`)
- **Question**: The Resident District Commissioner (RDC) represents the President and Central Government in a district.
- **Answer**: `true`
- **Hint**: The RDC is appointed directly by the President of Uganda.
- **Explanation**: The RDC is the senior presidential representative in the district, overseeing security and government government programs.
- **Deep Dive**: While the LC5 Chairperson is the elected political head of the district and the CAO is the highest civil servant accounting officer, the RDC monitors central government projects and chairs the District Security Committee.

### Question 4 (`multi_select`)
- **Question**: Which of the following are **cardinal points** of a compass? (Select all that apply)
- **Options**:
  - A. North (`true`)
  - B. North-East (`false`)
  - C. East (`true`)
  - D. South (`true`)
- **Explanation**: North, South, East, and West are the cardinal points. North-East is an intermediate (secondary) point between North and East.

## 3. P4 Integrated Science: Flowering Plants & Germination (`p4-sci-plants`)

### Question 1 (`multiple_choice`)
- **Question**: Which part of a flowering plant is responsible for making food through photosynthesis?
- **Options**:
  - A. Roots (`false`)
  - B. Stem (`false`)
  - C. Leaves (`true`)
  - D. Flower (`false`)
- **Hint**: This part contains a green pigment called chlorophyll and traps sunlight.
- **Explanation**: Leaves carry out photosynthesis using sunlight, carbon dioxide, and water to produce food (sugar) for the plant.
- **Deep Dive**: During photosynthesis, leaves release oxygen into the atmosphere, which humans and animals breathe.

### Question 2 (`short_answer`)
- **Question**: What name is given to the process by which a dry seed develops into a young seedling?
- **Answer**: Germination
- **Hint**: It requires water, warmth, and oxygen.
- **Explanation**: Germination is the sprouting of a seed into a seedling when favourable conditions are met.
- **Deep Dive**: The three essential conditions for seed germination are moisture (water to soften the seed coat), warmth (favourable temperature for enzymes), and oxygen (for respiration). Seeds do NOT require sunlight to germinate!

### Question 3 (`matching`)
- **Question**: Match each plant part with its primary function.
- **Pairs**:
  - `pp1`: **Roots** $\rightarrow$ Absorb water and mineral salts from the soil
  - `pp2`: **Stem** $\rightarrow$ Transports water and supports branches and leaves
  - `pp3`: **Flower** $\rightarrow$ Serves as the reproductive organ of the plant
  - `pp4`: **Fruit** $\rightarrow$ Protects the developing seeds and aids dispersion
- **Explanation**: Every structure on a flowering plant works together to ensure survival and reproduction.

## 4. P4 English Language: Nouns, Plurals & Simple Tenses (`p4-eng-nouns`)

### Question 1 (`multiple_choice`)
- **Question**: Choose the correct **plural form** of the irregular noun **Child**.
- **Options**:
  - A. Childs (`false`)
  - B. Children (`true`)
  - C. Childrens (`false`)
  - D. Childes (`false`)
- **Hint**: This noun does not add 's' or 'es' to make its plural.
- **Explanation**: 'Child' is an irregular noun whose plural is 'Children'.
- **Deep Dive**: Other common irregular plurals in P4 English include: man $\rightarrow$ men, woman $\rightarrow$ women, tooth $\rightarrow$ teeth, foot $\rightarrow$ feet, ox $\rightarrow$ oxen, and mouse $\rightarrow$ mice.

### Question 2 (`short_answer`)
- **Question**: Complete the sentence with the correct past tense of the verb in brackets: "Yesterday, Akello ______ (go) to the village well to fetch clean water."
- **Answer**: went
- **Hint**: 'Go' is an irregular verb.
- **Explanation**: The simple past tense of 'go' is 'went'.
- **Deep Dive**: We use the simple past tense for completed actions in the past. Words like *yesterday, last week, ago, in 2024* indicate past tense.

### Question 3 (`true_false`)
- **Question**: In the sentence "Kampala is the capital city of Uganda," the words **Kampala** and **Uganda** are proper nouns.
- **Answer**: `true`
- **Hint**: Proper nouns name specific places or persons and must begin with capital letters.
- **Explanation**: Kampala and Uganda are specific names of a city and a country, so they are proper nouns and always capitalized.
