## Complete Curriculum Structure (`4 Master Topics -> 16 Modules -> 196 Questions`)

### 1. Punctuation, Vocabulary, Spelling & Affixes (`p5-eng-grammar-vocab`) - 4 Modules, 50 Questions
- **Module 1: Punctuation (Capitalization, Full Stops, Commas, Apostrophes, Direct Speech)** (`p5-eg-m1`, 12 Questions)
  - Proper noun capitalization (`names, days, towns like Gulu/Kampala`); Commas in lists (`separating items before 'and'`); Singular and plural possessive apostrophes (`Kintu's bag vs pupils' desks`); Contractions (`can't, won't, shan't, doesn't`); Direct speech punctuation (`quotation marks/inverted commas, commas after reporting verbs 'said,' and closing marks strictly inside quotes`); Semicolons and Title Case (`header capitalization`).
- **Module 2: Vocabulary & Word Relationships (Synonyms, Antonyms, Homophones, Homographs)** (`p5-eg-m2`, 12 Questions)
  - Synonyms (`concise/brief, enormous/huge/giant`); Antonyms (`permanent/temporary, mandatory/optional, postpone/bring forward`); Homophones (`there/their/they're, to/too/two, hear/here, meat/meet, flour/flower, peace/piece, allowed/aloud, whether/weather, stationary/stationery`); Homographs and Heteronyms (`lead/lead, bark/bark, row/row`); Alphabetical ordering up to the 4th letter (`Character, Charcoal, Chart, Chase`).
- **Module 3: Prefixes & Suffixes (Prefixes un-, dis-, re-; Suffixes -er, -ful, Abstract Nouns)** (`p5-eg-m3`, 13 Questions)
  - Negative prefixes (`impatient, disagree, illegal, irregular, inaccurate`); Reversal/repetition prefixes (`rewrite, rebuild, revisit, prepaid vs postpaid`); Adjective suffixes (`careful vs careless, happily with y-to-i change`); Abstract nouns (`honesty, decision from decide, movement, education, depth from deep with vowel change, truth from true, pride from proud`); Specialist agent suffixes (`-ist pianist/scientist, -er teacher, -or actor`).
- **Module 4: Standard Abbreviations, Plurals (Irregular/Compound) & Collective Nouns** (`p5-eg-m4`, 13 Questions)
  - Abbreviations (`e.g. for example, i.e. that is, Dr. Doctor, Mr., Mrs., P.T.O., R.S.V.P., NCDC, UNEB, P.O. Box`); Irregular plurals (`ox/oxen, child/children, mouse/mice, tooth/teeth, woman/women`); Compound hyphenated plurals (`mothers-in-law, fathers-in-law, passers-by, heads of school`); Invariable plurals (`sheep, deer, cattle, furniture, advice, news`); Latin/foreign plurals (`radius/radii, nucleus/nuclei, fungus/fungi, datum/data`); F-to-ves plurals (`leaf/leaves, thief/thieves, wife/wives vs exceptions chief/chiefs, cliff/cliffs`); Collective nouns (`pride of lions, bouquet of flowers, choir of singers, bench of judges, fleet of ships`).

### 2. Comprehensive English Grammar & Sentence Structures (`p5-eng-grammar-structures`) - 5 Modules, 61 Questions
- **Module 1: Verb Tenses & Irregular Conjugations (Present/Past Simple & Continuous, Perfect)** (`p5-gs-m1`, 12 Questions)
  - Present Simple (`daily routines, universal truths`); Past Simple irregulars (`catch/caught, buy/bought, run/ran`); Past Continuous (`interrupted actions: were planting when it rained`); Present Perfect (`has/have + Past Participle: driven, sung, rung, swum, written, broken`); Future Simple (`shall/will vs going to future intention`); Chronological tense sequencing.
- **Module 2: Subject-Verb Agreement, Pronouns & Articles (a, an, the, Reflexive Pronouns)** (`p5-gs-m2`, 12 Questions)
  - Correlative agreement (`neither...nor proximity rule: nor his brother attends`); Parenthetical phrases (`together with, as well as - agrees with first subject`); Articles (`a, an before vowel sounds like an honest man, an hour vs a university, the for unique objects like the sun`); Reflexive pronouns (`myself, yourself, himself, herself, ourselves, themselves`); Possessive pronouns (`mine, yours, hers`); Relative pronouns (`who for people subjects, whom for objects, whose for possession, which/that for objects`); Indefinite pronouns (`everyone, each taking singular verbs`); Demonstrative pronouns (`this/these near vs that/those far`); Uncountable nouns taking singular verbs (`news is, advice was`); Prepositions requiring object case (`between you and me`).
- **Module 3: Direct Speech vs Reported Speech (Tense Backshift, Pronoun & Time Adverb Shifts)** (`p5-gs-m3`, 12 Questions)
  - Direct to Reported speech transformations (`Akello said that she was going...`); Pronoun shifts (`I -> he/she, our -> their`); Tense backshift (`Present Simple -> Past Simple, Present Continuous -> Past Continuous, Present Perfect -> Past Perfect had finished`); Time/place adverb shifts (`tomorrow -> the next day / following day, yesterday -> the previous day, today -> that day, here -> there, this -> that`); Reported questions (`asked/inquired + statement word order without question marks`); Reported commands/requests (`ordered/warned + object + TO / NOT TO + base infinitive verb`); Universal truths exemption (`no backshift for geographical facts like the Equator`); Yes/No question connectors (`if / whether`); Modal backshifts (`can -> could, will -> would, may -> might, must -> had to`).
- **Module 4: Active Voice vs Passive Voice (Object -> Subject + to be + Past Participle)** (`p5-gs-m4`, 12 Questions)
  - Active vs Passive transformation (`Object becomes new subject + appropriate form of to be + Past Participle + by + Agent`); Present Simple Passive (`is/are prepared`); Past Simple Passive (`was/were destroyed`); Future Simple Passive (`will be built`); Present Continuous Passive (`is/are BEING washed`); Present Perfect Passive (`has/have BEEN completed`); Omitting obvious agents in scientific/historical reports (`Mengo Hospital was built in 1897`); Intransitive verb exemption (`slept, happened, arrived cannot form passive voice`); Reversing passive back to active.
- **Module 5: Question Tags, Conditionals (Type 1 & 2) & Degrees of Comparison (-er/more)** (`p5-gs-m5`, 13 Questions)
  - Question Tags (`positive statement -> negative tag isn't she?; negative statement -> positive tag did they?`); Exception tags (`I am early -> aren't I?; Let us play -> shall we?; Open the door -> will you?`); Negative frequency adverbs triggering positive tags (`never, rarely, hardly -> does he?`); Conditionals Type 1 (`Real/Likely: If + Present Simple, + Future Simple will pass`); Conditionals Type 2 (`Unlikely/Imaginary: If + Past Simple were/had, + would + base infinitive would construct - using 'were' for all persons`); Degrees of comparison (`Positive, Comparative -er/more than, Superlative the + -est/most`); Irregular comparisons (`good/better/best, bad/worse/worst, many/more/most, little/less/least`); Multi-syllable adjectives (`more beautiful / the most beautiful without double comparatives`); Double-correlative structures (`The higher... the colder...`); Equal/unequal comparison (`as tall as / not as tall as`); Y-ending comparative spelling (`heavy -> heavier`).

### 3. Reading Comprehension & Critical Analysis (`p5-eng-comprehension`) - 3 Modules, 36 Questions
- **Module 1: Continuous Prose Passages (Stories & Reports - Factual Recall & Inference)** (`p5-rc-m1`, 12 Questions)
  - Factual recall (`visiting Mr. Musoke's poultry farm in Gulu, 2,000 layers, ventilation preventing Newcastle/Coccidiosis`); Vocabulary inference from context (`well-ventilated, indomitable spirit of Dr. David Livingstone meaning unconquerable/unbeatable`); Main idea and paragraph summary (`Karamoja dry season water scarcity and solar boreholes`); Logical inference (`why solar boreholes solve dry season crises`); Chronological narrative sequencing (`Kintu's bus journey from village across Nile bridge to Kampala`); Active reading strategy (`skimming first, reading questions, scanning for exact facts`); Explaining 'Why' questions with complete factual reasons (`Because... / To...`).
- **Module 2: Non-Continuous Passages (Timetables, Menus, Notices, Advertisements & Tables)** (`p5-rc-m2`, 12 Questions)
  - Bus/train timetables (`calculating exact travel durations across stopovers: Kampala to Karuma Falls 4 hours, arrival at Gulu Terminal 1:45 PM / 13:45 hours`); Restaurant price menus (`Owino Food Court bill summation and cost-saving subtraction UGX 13,500 vs UGX 3,500 saved`); Public notices and announcements (`PTA mandatory attendance rules, verifying issuing signatory 'By Order of the Headteacher'`); Examination calendars (`identifying specific examination days of the week and time slots`); Job advertisements (`identifying vacancy titles, duties, qualifications, and submission deadlines`); Sports tournament tables (`calculating points from Wins $W \times 3$, Draws $D \times 1$, and Losses $L \times 0$ - e.g. Crane House 10 points`); Lost-and-found notices (`verifying item descriptions and return contacts`).
- **Module 3: Poetry & Rhyme Interpretation (Stanzas, Rhyme Schemes, Similes & Metaphors)** (`p5-rc-m3`, 12 Questions)
  - Stanza and line identification (`counting stanzas/verses as grouped blocks of lines`); Rhyme scheme mapping (`assigning letter codes A-A-B-B rhyming couplets like wide/pride and clear/year on the River Nile`); Similes (`direct comparisons using as...as or like: as loud as an angry lion, as fast as a cheetah, as brave as a lion, as proud as a peacock`); Metaphors (`direct comparisons stating one thing IS another: Mrs. Akello is a shining guiding star`); Persona (`identifying the speaker or voice inside the poem`); Personification (`giving human actions to nature: palm trees dancing, river roaring`); Poetry analytical steps (`Title -> Read steadily -> Check questions -> Scan exact line numbers`); Refrain / Chorus (`repeating key lines at the end of stanzas for emphasis and musical rhythm`); Free verse (`blank verse without end rhymes`).

### 4. Composition, Formal Letter Writing & Functional Writing (`p5-eng-composition`) - 4 Modules, 49 Questions
- **Module 1: Personal vs Official Letters (Structure, Addresses, Salutations & Sign-offs)** (`p5-cw-m1`, 12 Questions)
  - Structural contrast: Informal/Friendly letter (`ONE address top right + date, casual salutation Dear Uncle Kato,, closing Your loving son, Kintu without printed surname`) vs Formal/Official letter (`TWO addresses top right sender and left margin receiver, formal salutation Dear Sir/Madam, or Dear Mr. Musoke,, mandatory capitalized or underlined Reference line REF: REQUEST FOR SICK LEAVE between salutation and body`); Complimentary close rules (`Yours faithfully, for Dear Sir/Madam vs Yours sincerely, for exact surnames, followed strictly by signature + printed full name - spelling sincerely correctly and capitalizing Yours without apostrophes`); Tone and slang prohibition in formal letters; 7-part layout order; Punctuation styles (`Open block format without commas vs Closed traditional comma format`).
- **Module 2: Functional Writing: Notices, Announcements, Invitation Cards & Telegrams** (`p5-cw-m2`, 12 Questions)
  - Public Notices (`answering The 5 Ws: Who, What, Where venue, When date/time, Why purpose, plus issuing signature at bottom`); Lost-and-Found announcements (`exact physical item descriptions and return office/reward details`); Invitation Cards (`formal phrasing cordially invite / have the pleasure to invite, centered border frame layout, event details`); R.S.V.P. (`Repondez S'il Vous Plaît confirming attendance or regretting inability to attend before deadline`); Telegrams and emergency SMS (`ultra-concise all-caps keyword messages stripping out articles/greetings: ARRIVED SAFELY SCHOOL KAMPALA 2PM ALL WELL KINTU`).
- **Module 3: Dialogues, Conversations & Meeting Minutes (Colons, Stage Directions, A.O.B.)** (`p5-cw-m3`, 12 Questions)
  - Dialogue script formatting (`speaker name on left margin followed immediately by COLON : and spoken words without quotation marks, every new speaker starting on a fresh left-hand line`); Stage directions (`physical actions, emotions, or tone enclosed strictly inside Brackets / Parentheses () to separate them from dialogue`); Meeting Minutes (`official written record of discussions, attendance Present/Apologies/In Attendance, and agreed resolutions`); Agenda and structure (`Minute 1 Communication from Chair, Minute 2 Confirmation of Previous Minutes, Minute 3 Matters Arising from past resolutions, Minute 4/5 New Business, A.O.B. Any Other Business for minor announcements, Adjournment exact closing time, and bottom dual signatures of Chairperson and Secretary`).
- **Module 4: Guided & Picture Composition (Chronological Storytelling, Paragraphs, Connectors)** (`p5-cw-m4`, 13 Questions)
  - Picture composition strategy (`studying all 4 or 6 numbered pictures sequentially before writing to understand characters, setting, conflict, climax, and resolution without skipping scenes`); Transitional connectors (`firstly, next, then, afterwards, meanwhile, suddenly, as a result, eventually, finally - linking sentences and paragraphs smoothly`); Paragraphing (`dividing continuous essays into 3-4 distinct indented paragraphs around single scene ideas`); Tense consistency (`maintaining Past Simple strictly throughout past narratives`); Guided composition / cloze passages (`skimming passage first, selecting exact fitting words from word bank, re-reading to verify grammar and agreement`); Eliminating mother-tongue direct translation / L1 interference (`distinguishing lend giving vs borrow taking, smell an aroma vs hear a smell, reaching Kampala vs reaching at Kampala, hearing vs listening`); Sensory imagery (`using vivid adjectives/verbs appealing to Sight, Sound, Smell, Taste, Touch`); Proverbial story conclusions (`Look before you leap / Disobedience leads to tears`); Proofreading (`spending 5 minutes before submission to catch careless punctuation/spelling slips`).
