## 1. P7 Social Studies: Our Country Uganda (`39 Canonical UNEB Questions`)

### Session 1: Location, Physical Features & Administration (`p7-uganda-session-1`)
1. **How many countries border Uganda?** $\rightarrow$ **Five** (Kenya, South Sudan, DR Congo, Rwanda, Tanzania).
2. **Uganda is a landlocked country.** $\rightarrow$ **True** (No coastline; uses Mombasa and Dar es Salaam ports).
3. **Which country lies to the east of Uganda?** $\rightarrow$ **Kenya** (Border runs from Lake Victoria to South Sudan).
4. **The equator passes through which region of Uganda?** $\rightarrow$ **Central Region** (Kayabwe, Mpigi, Kasese).
5. **Match neighbouring country to direction:** Kenya $\rightarrow$ East, South Sudan $\rightarrow$ North, DR Congo $\rightarrow$ West, Tanzania $\rightarrow$ South.
6. **Which physical feature forms natural border between Uganda and DR Congo?** $\rightarrow$ **Mount Rwenzori & Lake Albert**.
7. **What is the highest mountain peak in Uganda?** $\rightarrow$ **Margherita Peak** (5,109m on Mount Rwenzori).
8. **Lake Victoria is a Rift Valley lake.** $\rightarrow$ **False** (It is a down-warped basin lake between two rift branches).
9. **Name the longest river in Uganda that flows out of Lake Victoria.** $\rightarrow$ **River Nile** (Flows north to Mediterranean Sea).
10. **Order physical features by height (highest to lowest):** Mount Rwenzori (5109m), Mount Elgon (4321m), Mount Moroto (3083m), Lake Victoria basin (1134m).
11. **Who is political head of a district in Uganda?** $\rightarrow$ **LC5 Chairperson** (Elected five-year term).
12. **Match administrative unit to political head:** Village $\rightarrow$ LC1 Chairperson, Parish $\rightarrow$ LC2 Chairperson, Sub-county $\rightarrow$ LC3 Chairperson, District $\rightarrow$ LC5 Chairperson.

### Session 2: Economy, Tourism, Climate & Vegetation (`p7-uganda-session-2`)
1. **What is Uganda's main cash crop?** $\rightarrow$ **Coffee** (Robusta and Arabica export leader).
2. **Name one mineral mined in Kasese district.** $\rightarrow$ **Copper** (At Kilembe mines; plus cobalt).
3. **Important export from Uganda?** $\rightarrow$ **Coffee** (Alongside tea, fish, flowers, gold).
4. **Uganda exports oil to other countries.** $\rightarrow$ **False** (Crude reserves discovered in Albertine Graben; refinery/pipeline under development).
5. **Match economic activity to region/town:** Fishing $\rightarrow$ Kalangala (Lake Victoria), Tourism $\rightarrow$ Bwindi Impenetrable Forest, Mining $\rightarrow$ Kilembe (Kasese), Industrial manufacturing $\rightarrow$ Namanve/Jinja.
6. **Which national park in Uganda is famous for mountain gorillas?** $\rightarrow$ **Bwindi Impenetrable National Park**.
7. **Tourism is an invisible export.** $\rightarrow$ **True** (Foreign tourists bring foreign exchange without physical goods leaving).
8. **Name the biggest national park in Uganda.** $\rightarrow$ **Murchison Falls National Park** (Crossed by River Nile).
9. **Order Uganda's revenue sources:** Agricultural exports $\rightarrow$ Tourism & hospitality $\rightarrow$ Industrial manufacturing $\rightarrow$ Mineral processing.
10. **Type of climate experienced in most parts of Uganda?** $\rightarrow$ **Tropical climate** (Wet and dry seasons with warm temperatures).
11. **Natural vegetation covering most of Uganda?** $\rightarrow$ **Savannah grassland** (Acacia trees and tall grass).
12. **Match vegetation type to characteristic:** Tropical rainforest $\rightarrow$ Tall dense evergreen trees with buttress roots, Savannah grassland $\rightarrow$ Grass with scattered acacia trees, Swamp/wetland $\rightarrow$ Papyrus reeds and waterlogged soil, Mountain vegetation $\rightarrow$ Heath and bamboo changing with altitude.

### Session 3: People & Culture, Towns & History (`p7-uganda-session-3`)
1. **Largest ethnic group in Uganda?** $\rightarrow$ **Baganda** (Bantu group living in Central Uganda).
2. **Official language used in schools/government?** $\rightarrow$ **English** (Swahili is second official language).
3. **Traditional food of the Baganda?** $\rightarrow$ **Matooke** (Steamed and mashed green bananas).
4. **Luganda spoken by majority in central Uganda.** $\rightarrow$ **True** (Widely spoken trade/social language).
5. **Match ethnic group to classification:** Baganda $\rightarrow$ Bantu, Acholi $\rightarrow$ River-Lake Nilotes, Karamojong $\rightarrow$ Plain Nilotes, Lugbara $\rightarrow$ Sudanic.
6. **First capital city of Uganda during colonial times?** $\rightarrow$ **Entebbe** (On shores of Lake Victoria before move to Kampala).
7. **Important industrial town in eastern Uganda near Owen Falls Dam?** $\rightarrow$ **Jinja** (Source of the Nile).
8. **Kampala is both capital city and largest commercial centre.** $\rightarrow$ **True** (Host to Parliament, ministries, markets).
9. **Order historical capital locations chronologically:** Entebbe (1894 Protectorate HQ) $\rightarrow$ Kampala (1962 Independence Capital) $\rightarrow$ Modern Greater Kampala Metropolitan Area.
10. **Who was the first Prime Minister of independent Uganda in 1962?** $\rightarrow$ **Dr. Apollo Milton Obote**.
11. **Uganda gained independence from Britain on 9th October 1962.** $\rightarrow$ **True** (Sir Edward Mutesa became first ceremonial President in 1963).
12. **Name the British explorer who first saw Lake Victoria in 1862 and named it.** $\rightarrow$ **John Hanning Speke**.
13. **Match historical figure to role:** Kabaka Mutesa I $\rightarrow$ Invited Christian missionaries in 1875 letter, Captain Frederick Lugard $\rightarrow$ Raised British flag in Buganda (1890), Sir Hesketh Bell $\rightarrow$ First British representative in Buganda, Apollo Milton Obote $\rightarrow$ First Executive Prime Minister (1962).
14. **Why was Bwindi Impenetrable Forest listed as UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1994?** $\rightarrow$ **Importance for mountain gorilla conservation**.
15. **Name the river that forms part of border between Uganda and Tanzania.** $\rightarrow$ **River Kagera** (Flows into Lake Victoria).

## 2. P7 Mathematics: PLE UNEB Business Math & Geometry (`p7-math-business`)

### Question 1 (`multiple_choice`)
- **Question**: A businessman deposited **UGX 400,000** in a commercial bank that offers simple interest at a rate of **5% per annum**. How much **simple interest** will he earn after **2 years**?
- **Options**:
  - A. UGX 20,000 (`false`)
  - B. UGX 40,000 (`true`)
  - C. UGX 80,000 (`false`)
  - D. UGX 440,000 (`false`)
- **Hint**: Use the simple interest formula: $I = \frac{P \times R \times T}{100}$.
- **Explanation**: $I = \frac{400,000 \times 5 \times 2}{100} = \frac{4,000,000}{100} = \text{UGX } 40,000$.
- **Deep Dive**: Total amount accumulated after 2 years ($A = P + I$) would be $400,000 + 40,000 = \text{UGX } 440,000$.

### Question 2 (`short_answer`)
- **Question**: The area of a rectangular school football pitch is **1,200 square meters**. If its width is **30 meters**, what is its length in meters?
- **Answer**: 40
- **Hint**: $\text{Length} = \frac{\text{Area}}{\text{Width}}$.
- **Explanation**: $\frac{1200}{30} = 40\text{ meters}$.

## 3. P7 Integrated Science: Energy & Environment (`p7-sci-energy`)

### Question 1 (`multiple_choice`)
- **Question**: Which of the following is a **renewable source of energy** widely used in Uganda?
- **Options**:
  - A. Coal (`false`)
  - B. Hydroelectric power (`true`)
  - C. Petroleum (`false`)
  - D. Natural gas (`false`)
- **Hint**: It is generated by rushing water at dams like Owen Falls, Nalubaale, Bujagali, and Karuma on the River Nile.
- **Explanation**: Hydroelectric power, solar energy, and biogas are renewable because they are naturally replenished.

### Question 2 (`short_answer`)
- **Question**: Which human organ removes urea, excess salts, and water from the blood in the form of urine?
- **Answer**: Kidneys
- **Hint**: They are bean-shaped organs in the lower back.
- **Explanation**: The kidneys filter blood to produce urine, maintaining body fluid balance (osmoregulation).

## 4. P7 English Language: PLE Composition & Vocabulary (`p7-eng-composition`)

### Question 1 (`multiple_choice`)
- **Question**: Choose the correctly punctuated **formal salutation** when writing a letter to a Bank Manager whose name you do not know.
- **Options**:
  - A. Dear Bank Manager, (`false`)
  - B. Dear Sir/Madam, (`true`)
  - C. My dear Sir, (`false`)
  - D. Hello Manager (`false`)
- **Hint**: When you do not know the recipient's personal name, use the standard formal greeting.
- **Explanation**: *Dear Sir/Madam,* is the official salutation for formal letters, and such letters always close with *Yours faithfully,*.

### Question 2 (`short_answer`)
- **Question**: Complete the sentence with the correct form of the word in brackets: "The PLE candidates walked ______ (quiet) into the examination hall."
- **Answer**: quietly
- **Hint**: Change the adjective to an adverb of manner.
- **Explanation**: *Quietly* describes *how* the candidates walked into the hall.
