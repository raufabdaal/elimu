## Master Topic Breakdown & Module Inventory

### 1. Plant Life & Flowering Plants (`p4-sci-plants` - 3 Modules, 36 Questions)
- **Module 1: Parts of a Flowering Plant & Their Functions** (`p4-sci-plants-m1`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers root systems (`taproot vs fibrous roots`), root hair absorption, stem transport (`xylem vs phloem`), leaf photosynthesis (`stomata, chlorophyll, sunlight, carbon dioxide`), transpiration pull, and phototropism.
- **Module 2: The Flower, Pollination & Fertilization** (`p4-sci-plants-m2`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers floral structure (`petals, sepals, calyx, corolla`), male organs (`stamen: anther & filament`), female organs (`carpel/pistil: stigma, style, ovary, ovules`), self vs cross pollination (`wind vs insect adaptations`), honeybee pollination, and fruit/seed development.
- **Module 3: Types of Plants, Adaptations & Economic Uses** (`p4-sci-plants-m3`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers monocotyledons (`maize, parallel veins`) vs dicotyledons (`beans, network veins`), climbers and tendrils, leguminous root nodules (`Rhizobium nitrogen fixation`), hydrophytes (`water lilies`), desert xerophytes (`cactus thorns`), insectivorous plants (`pitcher plant`), storage tubers (`cassava vs Irish potatoes`), Arabica coffee, and reforestation.

### 2. Crop Growing & Seed Germination (`p4-sci-crops-germination` - 3 Modules, 36 Questions)
- **Module 1: Seed Structure & Conditions for Germination** (`p4-sci-crops-m1`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers seed anatomy (`testa/seed coat, hilum scar, micropyle pore, cotyledon/scutellum, plumule shoot, radicle root`), the three mandatory conditions (`Moisture, Warmth, Oxygen`), enzyme activation, danger of boiling/roasting seeds, and seed dormancy.
- **Module 2: Types of Germination & Caring for Seedlings** (`p4-sci-crops-m2`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers epigeal germination (`beans, arched hypocotyl pushing cotyledons above ground`) vs hypogeal germination (`maize, coleoptile sheath, cotyledons remaining below ground`), nursery bed shades (`filtering sun/rain`), watering, thinning, pricking out, hardening off, evening transplanting, and mulching.
- **Module 3: Garden Tools, Land Preparation & Crop Pests** (`p4-sci-crops-m3`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers essential tools (`hand hoe/jembe, rake, panga, garden trowel, wheelbarrow, watering can`), tool maintenance (`washing, drying, oiling against rust`), primary cultivation (`turning humus`), crop rotation (`breaking pest cycles`), destructive pests (`maize stalk borers, armyworms, sweet potato weevils, aphids`), weeds (`blackjack, couch grass`), and timely harvest drying (`preventing aflatoxins & weevils`).

### 3. Personal Hygiene & Sanitation (`p4-sci-hygiene-sanitation` - 3 Modules, 36 Questions)
- **Module 1: Body Cleanliness, Grooming & Sensory Organs** (`p4-sci-hygiene-m1`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers daily bathing (`removing skin bacteria & sweat`), twice-daily teeth brushing, trimming short fingernails/toenails (`preventing worm eggs`), care of eyes (`Vitamin A, good lighting`), ears (`no sharp sticks inside canal`), nose (`cilia filtration`), tippy-tap handwashing (`20 seconds with soap and running water`), and danger of sharing razors/towels/combs (`ringworm, Hepatitis B, HIV`).
- **Module 2: Germs, Vectors & Preventing Common Infections** (`p4-sci-hygiene-m2`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers Plasmodium malaria parasite, vectors (`Anopheles mosquito, housefly mechanical transmission via hairy legs, tsetse fly sleeping sickness, Aedes yellow fever`), boiling drinking water against cholera/typhoid, integrated malaria control (`ITNs, draining puddles, bush clearing, oiling`), complete metamorphosis (`Egg -> Larva -> Pupa -> Adult`), ORS preparation (`1 L water + 6 tsp sugar + 1/2 tsp salt against dehydration`), antibiotics (`bacteria only, not viruses`), airborne tuberculosis (`cough hygiene`), and helminth deworming.
- **Module 3: Home Sanitation, Waste Disposal & Latrines** (`p4-sci-hygiene-m3`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers pit latrine siting (`at least 30 m away from wells`), drop-hole covers and VIP latrine vent pipes (`trapping flies & venting odors`), sorting biodegradable kitchen scraps (`compost pit turning into organic manure`) from non-biodegradable plastics (`kaveera blocking drainage & releasing dioxins`), elevated plate drying racks (`sun UV sterilization away from dogs`), and stopping open defecation (`preventing cholera epidemics`).

### 4. Weather, Air & Atmosphere (`p4-sci-weather-air` - 3 Modules, 36 Questions)
- **Module 1: Elements of Weather & Local Weather Instruments** (`p4-sci-weather-m1`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers rain gauge (`measuring rainfall in mm, placed in open grass away from trees`), wind vane (`arrow pointing into wind direction`), anemometer (`spinning cups measuring wind speed in km/h`), Stevenson screen (`white reflecting paint, louvered slatted sides for true shade air temperature`), Campbell-Stokes sunshine recorder (`burning trace on card`), windsocks at airports, and maximum/minimum thermometers (`mercury vs alcohol`).
- **Module 2: Composition, Properties & Importance of Air** (`p4-sci-weather-m2`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers exact atmospheric percentages (`Nitrogen 78%, Oxygen 21%, Argon/Inert 0.97%, Carbon dioxide 0.03%`), oxygen supporting respiration & combustion (`candle inside inverted jar rising water level`), air occupying space (`balloon`), air having weight (`weighing football`), mechanical mixture properties, collapsing-tin atmospheric pressure demonstration (`101.3 kPa`), carbon dioxide turning lime water milky white, thin hypoxic mountain air (`high altitude oxygen density`), and water vapour.
- **Module 3: Wind, Clouds & The Water Cycle in Our Environment** (`p4-sci-weather-m3`) - 12 Questions
  - Covers the Water Cycle (`Evaporation/Transpiration -> Rising/Cooling -> Condensation -> Precipitation`), wind (`air moving from high to low pressure due to uneven solar heating`), sea/lake breezes vs land breezes, major cloud types (`Cirrus ice wisps, Cumulus fair cotton balls, Stratus low grey drizzle blankets, Cumulonimbus towering dark thunderheads bringing rain & hail`), tree windbreaks (`stopping mechanical wind damage & topsoil erosion`), condensation (`morning dew & exhaled mist in cool highlands`), forest transpiration (`Mabira forest rainfall pumps`), and traditional grain winnowing.
